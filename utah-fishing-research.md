## Key Utah Fishing Locations

### 1. Strawberry Reservoir

**Primary Species:**
- Bear Lake Cutthroat Trout (16-20" average, 24"+ trophy fish)
- Rainbow Trout (sterile triploids, fast-growing)
- Kokanee Salmon

**Best Fishing Depths by Season:**
| Species | Spring/Fall | Summer | Winter |
|---------|-------------|--------|--------|
| Cutthroat | 25-50 ft | 25-50 ft | 15-25 ft (over 40 ft of water) |
| Rainbow | 20-35 ft | 20-35 ft | 6-15 ft near shoreline |
| Kokanee | 10-20 ft (early AM) | 30-50 ft | 20-50 ft (later in day) |

**Best Times:**
- **Ice-off (mid-May):** Prime time - hungry trout feed aggressively near banks
- **January-February:** Excellent ice fishing (8-12" ice typical)
- **Kokanee Season:** Mid-May through July (before August spawning)

**Regulations (2026):**
- Cutthroat slot rule: Release all 15-22", only 1 over 22" allowed
- Combined trout/kokanee limit: 4 fish

### 2. Flaming Gorge Reservoir

**Primary Species:**
- Lake Trout (trophy water - fish over 50 lbs caught regularly)
- Kokanee Salmon
- Rainbow Trout
- Smallmouth Bass
- Brown Trout
- Burbot
- Channel Catfish

**Best Fishing Depths by Season:**
| Species | Spring | Summer | Fall | Winter |
|---------|--------|--------|------|--------|
| Lake Trout | Near surface (castable) | 70-120 ft (typically 80-100 ft) | 50-100 ft | 70-120 ft |
| Rainbow | 15-50 ft (suspended) | 15-50 ft | 15-50 ft | Variable |
| Kokanee | 0-40 ft | 40-50 ft | 40-60 ft | 0-40 ft |
| Smallmouth | Shallow (spawning) | Deep (bottom) | Deep | N/A |
| Burbot | 30-40 ft | 30-40 ft | 30-40 ft | 30-40 ft |

**Best Times:**
- **Spring (April-early May):** Excellent overall; lake trout accessible near surface
- **Early June:** Smallmouth spawn along rocky shorelines - fast action
- **Mid-August to September:** Kokanee stage in Sheep Creek Bay
- **Winter:** Upper reservoir freezes early January; good ice fishing

**Best Times of Day:** Morning and evening for lake trout; burbot best at night

- Brown Trout
- Walleye
- Smallmouth Bass
- Largemouth Bass
- Yellow Perch
- Bluegill

**Classification:** Blue Ribbon Fishery

**Best Seasons:**

### 4. Provo River

**Primary Species:**
- Brown Trout (12-16" average)
- Rainbow Trout
- Brook Trout (Upper section)
- Cutthroat Trout (Upper section)

**Sections & Best Times:**
| Section | Fish Density | Best Season | Notes |
|---------|--------------|-------------|-------|
| Middle Provo | 2,500-3,500 fish/mile | Year-round | Most consistent |
| Upper Provo | Moderate | July-September | Post-runoff; freestone stream |
| Lower Provo | Trophy water | March-April evenings | Largest average fish in Utah |

**Key Hatches:**
- **Spring:** Blue Winged Olives (BWOs), midges
- **June-July:** Green Drakes, Brown Drakes
- **Summer:** Terrestrials
- **Winter:** Midges and BWOs (11am-2pm window)

**Critical Temperature Rule:** Stop fishing if water exceeds 68°F

### 5. Green River (Below Flaming Gorge Dam)

**Primary Species:**
- Rainbow Trout
- Brown Trout (large specimens)

**Best Seasons:**
- **Spring (March-May):** Most productive; BWO hatches peak mid-late April
- **May:** Excellent before high water (typically May 20th); cicada hatches begin
- **Summer/Fall:** Streamer fishing for large browns and rainbows

**Major Hatches:**
- Blue Wing Olives (#16-18)
- Midges (#20-26, especially winter)
- Black Stoneflies (#14)
- Terrestrials (grasshoppers, ants, cicadas)

**Note:** Technical tailwater with educated fish requiring precise hatch matching

**Primary Species:**
- Channel Catfish (fish exceeding 20 lbs)
- White Bass (highly prolific)
- Walleye
- Largemouth Bass
- Bluegill
- Black Crappie
- Yellow Perch

**Best Seasons:**
| Season | Target Species | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Winter | White bass, perch, crappie, bluegill | Lincoln Beach warm spring area; ice fishing in marinas |
| Spring | White bass (May spawning run in Provo River), walleye | Shallow waters, rocky structures |
| Summer | Channel catfish, white bass | Peak catfish activity |
| Fall | White bass | Fast-paced action continues |

**Best Locations:**
- Provo Bay (vegetated - good for largemouth bass)
- Lincoln Beach (warm spring - winter fishing)
- Provo Boat Harbor, Lindon Marina, American Fork Harbor

**Primary Species:**

- Cutthroat Trout
- Splake
- Smallmouth Bass

- Walleye
- Yellow Perch

### 8. Pineview Reservoir

**Primary Species:**
- Tiger Muskie (apex predator - fish over 36" common; state record 49")
- Smallmouth Bass
- Yellow Perch (most numerous)
- Black Crappie
- Rainbow Trout
- Brown Trout
- Bullhead Catfish

**Best Seasons:**
| Season | Target Species | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Spring (April-June) | Tiger muskie, crappie | Crappie spawn shallow in May |
| Fall | Tiger muskie | Heavy feeding as temps drop |
| Winter | Yellow perch | Strong ice fishing |

**Regulations:**
- Tiger Muskie: CLOSED to possession (catch-and-release only)
- Bass: Limit 6, only 1 may exceed 12"
- Yellow Perch: Limit 50
- Black Crappie: Limit 20

## General Fishing Factors

### Barometric Pressure Effects

**Optimal Range:** 29.80 - 30.20 inHg (normal atmospheric: 29.70-30.40 inHg)

| Pressure Range | Fish Activity | Fishing Quality |
|----------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Above 30.50 inHg | Lethargic, near cover/deep water | Challenging |
| 30.20-30.50 inHg | Reduced feeding, sluggish | Fair |
| **29.80-30.20 inHg** | **Natural feeding behavior** | **Best** |
| 29.60-29.80 inHg | Increased activity | Good |
| Below 29.60 inHg | Unpredictable | Variable |
| Below 29.20 inHg | Unreliable | Poor |

**Pressure Trends (often more important than absolute reading):**
- **Falling Pressure:** Triggers aggressive feeding, especially before storms. Fish sense discomfort as swim bladders expand and feed actively while moving deeper.
- **Rising Pressure:** Compresses swim bladders, generally reduces feeding activity.
- **Stable Pressure:** Most consistent, predictable fishing with moderate feeding patterns.

---

### Moon Phase Effects (Solunar Theory)

**Daily Feeding Periods:**
| Period Type | Duration | Trigger | Intensity |
|-------------|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Major Periods** | 60-90 minutes | Moon directly overhead or underfoot | Highest |
| **Minor Periods** | 30-45 minutes | Moonrise and moonset | Moderate-High |

**Moon Phase Impact:**
- **Full Moon & New Moon:** Strongest overall fish activity
- **Quarter Moons:** Weakest activity

**Enhanced Fishing Conditions:**
- Major/minor period coincides with sunrise or sunset
- Moon transitioning to full or new phase
- Both major and minor periods occur on same day

---

### Wind Effects on Fishing

**Wind Direction:**
| Wind Type | Effect | Fishing Strategy |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Warm South/Southwest | Raises water temp, increases oxygen | Fish windward (onshore) side |
| Cold North/East | Chills surface water | Fish leeward (sheltered) side |
| Onshore (into bank) | Pushes baitfish inshore, concentrates predators | Best conditions |
| Offshore | Pushes food sources away | Less productive |

**Wind Speed:**
- **Moderate, steady wind:** Beneficial - stirs up food, breaks light penetration, oxygenates water
- **Strong wind:** Safety hazard, impractical conditions
- **No wind:** Fish may be more wary due to clear water/light penetration

---

### Best Fishing Hours

**Peak Times:**
| Time Period | Duration | Why It Works |
|-------------|----------|--------------|
| **Dawn** | 1 hour before to 2 hours after sunrise | Cooler water, low light, prey less coordinated, elevated dissolved oxygen |
| **Dusk** | 2 hours before to 1 hour after sunset | Often outperforms dawn; extended feeding window as fish make final feeding push |

**Midday Fishing (can be productive when):**
- Cold months (late fall through early spring) - sunlight warms water
- Heavy cloud cover
- Deep water fishing
- Targeting catfish

**Night Fishing (effective for):**
- Catfish
- Walleye
- Burbot
